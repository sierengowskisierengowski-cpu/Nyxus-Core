Configuration Guide (Where everything goes)1. The Glass & Borders (hyprland.conf)This goes in your main Hyprland configuration file. It sets the "physical" rules for your windows.ini# --- Section: General (Borders & Gaps) ---
general {
    gaps_in = 10
    gaps_out = 15
    border_size = 1
    col.active_border = rgba(d1d1d188) # The thin pencil line
    col.inactive_border = rgba(31313144) # The faded background line
}

# --- Section: Decoration (The Frost/Blur) ---
decoration {
    rounding = 15
    active_opacity = 0.85
    inactive_opacity = 0.70

    blur {
        enabled = true
        size = 12
        passes = 4
        brightness = 1.4    # Forces the "Frosted White" look
        vibrancy = 0.2      # Low vibrancy keeps it "Seattle Misty"
        noise = 0.04        # Adds the physical glass texture
    }
}

Detailed Configuration MapFollow this list to place the correct settings in the correct files.File: ~/.config/hypr/hyprland.confThis is the heart of your system. It defines the "material" of the glass and the "sketch" of the boxes.The "Sketch" Geometry (general block):gaps_in = 10 & gaps_out = 20: Large gaps to show off the wallpaper's texture.border_size = 1: Keeps borders as thin as a pencil line.col.active_border = rgba(d1d1d188): A light silver/grey stroke.col.inactive_border = rgba(31313144): A faded charcoal stroke.The "Milk Frost" Physics (decoration block):rounding = 15: Softens the tiled boxes.active_opacity = 0.85: Lets the faded black and white bleeds show through.blur:size = 12 & blur:passes = 4: Creates thick, premium glass.blur:brightness = 1.4: The key setting for the Frosted White look.blur:noise = 0.04: Adds the "grainy" physical glass texture.File: ~/.config/kitty/kitty.conf (or your Terminal)This handles the "Faded Black" system-wide base.background #1a1a1a: Use a faded charcoal rather than pure black.background_opacity 0.7: Allows the background mist to bleed through the black.File: ~/.config/waybar/style.css (The Bar)This makes your top bar look like a solid piece of white frosted glass.background: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.1);: A very faint white tint.font-family: "Architects Daughter";: Sets the hand-written display font.3. Typography GuideTo avoid a "sloppy" look, stick to this two-font rule:Display (The Accents): Use a handwritten font like Architects Daughter or Indie Flower for your clock, workspace numbers, and window titles.System (The Text): Use a clean, light-weight font like Inter Light or JetBrains Mono for your actual terminal text and coding. This keeps the system readable while the "sketch" accents provide the style.
